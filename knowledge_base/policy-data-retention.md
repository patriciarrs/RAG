# Data Retention & Deletion Policy

> **Audience:** All engineers, SREs, and anyone building features that store or process customer data.
> **Purpose:** Define how long AetherFlow retains customer data, what must be deleted and when, and the technical mechanisms that enforce these rules.
> **Related Docs:** `policy-secret-management.md`, `adr-005-database-postgres.md`, `reference-service-catalog.md`, `runbook-incident-response.md`

This policy exists for two reasons: **legal compliance** (GDPR, CCPA, and our customer DPAs) and **operational hygiene** (unbounded data growth is a reliability and cost risk). Every engineer building a feature that persists data is responsible for understanding where their data lands and how long it lives.

If you are unsure whether a new data store or field is covered by this policy, ask in `#security-compliance` before shipping.

## 1. Data Classification

Before applying a retention rule, identify what class of data you are handling. Classification determines both the retention period and the deletion method required.

| Class | Definition | Examples | Deletion Method |
|-------|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **PII** | Any data that directly identifies or can reasonably be used to identify a natural person | User email, name, IP address, device ID, user-agent string | Hard delete or irreversible anonymization |
| **Customer Event Data** | Raw and enriched event payloads submitted by tenants via the ingestion API | Arbitrary JSON in `ingestion_events.payload` | Hard delete per tenant agreement or DPA |
| **Operational Metadata** | Internal system data generated by AetherFlow (not submitted by customers) | Kafka consumer offsets, deployment logs, Prometheus metrics | Soft delete or TTL expiry |
| **Billing Records** | Invoice history, usage records, payment metadata | `billing_records`, Stripe payment intents | Retain 7 years (financial regulation); anonymize PII fields after contract end |
| **Audit Logs** | Immutable record of who accessed or modified what | `audit_log` table, Vault access logs, CloudTrail | Retain 2 years; cannot be deleted on customer request (regulatory exception) |
| **Security Logs** | Authentication events, failed logins, token issuance | auth-service logs, PagerDuty incident history | Retain 2 years |

### PII Fields — Current Inventory

The following fields across AetherFlow's PostgreSQL schema are classified as PII. Any new PII field must be added to this list via a PR to this document before the migration is merged:

| Table | Column | PII Type | Retention |
|-------|--------|---------|-----------|
| `tenants` | `admin_email` | Email address | Duration of contract + 90 days |
| `tenants` | `billing_email` | Email address | 7 years (billing records) |
| `user_sessions` | `ip_address` | IP address | 30 days from session end |
| `user_sessions` | `user_agent` | Device fingerprint | 30 days from session end |
| `audit_log` | `actor_ip` | IP address | 2 years (regulatory; non-deletable) |
| `ingestion_events` | `payload` | Arbitrary (tenant-controlled) | Per tenant DPA (default: 90 days) |

> Tenant event payloads are a special case. We do not know what PII a tenant includes in their event payloads — it is their responsibility under our Terms of Service to obtain consent for data they send us. However, our DPA commits us to honoring deletion requests for payload data. The `payload` column in `ingestion_events` must be deletable per tenant on request.

## 2. Retention Schedule by Storage Layer

### PostgreSQL (Primary Store)

| Table / Data | Default Retention | Configurable Per Tenant? | Enforcement Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| `ingestion_events` | **90 days** | Yes (DPA: up to 2 years) | Nightly deletion job |
| `metrics` (TimescaleDB) | **90 days** | No | TimescaleDB data retention policy |
| `metric_rollups_hourly` | **1 year** | No | TimescaleDB retention policy |
| `metric_rollups_daily` | **3 years** | No | TimescaleDB retention policy |
| `user_sessions` | **30 days** from session end | No | Nightly deletion job |
| `audit_log` | **2 years** | No — regulatory minimum | Partitioned table; monthly partition drop |
| `billing_records` | **7 years** | No — financial regulation | Manual review before deletion |
| `tenants` (active) | Indefinite while active | — | — |
| `tenants` (churned) | **90 days** post-offboarding | — | Offboarding job (see Section 4) |

### Kafka

Kafka retention is configured at the topic level. Default is 7 days for all topics. Replay topics have extended retention:

| Topic | Retention | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| `aetherflow.ingestion.raw-event.v1` | **7 days** | Replay window for stream-processor recovery |
| `aetherflow.ingestion.enriched-event.v1` | **7 days** | Downstream consumer replay |
| `aetherflow.analytics.aggregated-metric.v2` | **30 days** | Analytics replay window |
| `aetherflow.billing.usage-record.v1` | **90 days** | Billing audit trail |
| `aetherflow.tenants.config.changelog` | **Infinite** (compacted) | Source of truth for tenant config |
| `aetherflow.notifications.failed.v1` | **30 days** | Manual review window |

> **Important:** Kafka retention is separate from PostgreSQL retention. A customer event deleted from `ingestion_events` in PostgreSQL may still exist in the `raw-event.v1` Kafka topic for up to 7 days. For GDPR erasure requests, the 30-day completion window accounts for this overlap. See Section 3.

### S3 (Event Archive)

Raw events are archived to S3 (`aetherflow-prod-raw-event-archive`) for cold storage access and compliance audit purposes.

| Bucket / Prefix | Retention | Storage Class Transition |
|-----------------|-----------|--------------------------|
| `s3://aetherflow-prod-raw-event-archive/<tenant_id>/` | **1 year** | Standard to Glacier after 90 days |
| `s3://aetherflow-prod-audit-logs/` | **2 years** | Standard to Glacier after 30 days |
| `s3://aetherflow-prod-terraform-state-prod/` | Indefinite | Standard |

S3 lifecycle rules are managed via Terraform in `infra/environments/production/s3-lifecycle.tf`.

### Observability Signals (Datadog / Tempo / Prometheus / Loki)

| Signal | Retention | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Datadog APM traces | **15 days** | Datadog plan default |
| Grafana Tempo traces | **7 days** | Self-hosted; controlled by Tempo retention config |
| Prometheus metrics | **90 days** | `--storage.tsdb.retention.time=90d` |
| Loki logs | **30 days** | Configured in `helm-charts/monitoring/loki-values.yaml` |

Observability signals may contain PII (IP addresses in logs, tenant IDs in traces). These are operational PII and fall under the audit log exception — retained for their full observability window and not subject to individual Right to Erasure requests, as they form part of our security and audit infrastructure.

## 3. Customer-Initiated Deletion (Right to Erasure)

Under GDPR Article 17 and CCPA, users have the right to request deletion of their personal data. At AetherFlow, erasure requests come in two forms:

### Form A: User-Level Deletion (individual user within a tenant)

A tenant's end-user requests deletion of their data. The tenant submits this on behalf of their user via the AetherFlow API.

**Completion SLA: 30 days** from request receipt.

The deletion is triggered via an internal API endpoint:

```
POST /internal/v1/tenants/{tenant_id}/erasure-requests
Body: { "subject_type": "user", "subject_id": "user-uuid-123" }
```

This queues a job that:
1. Soft-deletes the `user_session` rows for that user (`deleted_at = NOW()`)
2. NULLs or pseudonymizes PII fields in `ingestion_events` where `payload->>'user_id'` matches
3. Writes an erasure completion record to the `audit_log`
4. The hard delete runs on the next nightly deletion job cycle (within 24 hours)

### Form B: Full Tenant Data Deletion

A tenant terminates their contract and requests deletion of all their data. See Section 4 for the full offboarding procedure.

### Erasure Request Tracking

All erasure requests are tracked in the `erasure_requests` PostgreSQL table and in Jira (`SEC` project, label: `erasure-request`). The Security team reviews open requests weekly. Requests not completed within 25 days trigger an automatic escalation to the VP of Engineering.

```sql
CREATE TABLE erasure_requests (
    id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    tenant_id       UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES tenants(id),
    subject_type    TEXT NOT NULL CHECK (subject_type IN ('user', 'tenant')),
    subject_id      TEXT NOT NULL,
    requested_at    TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW(),
    completed_at    TIMESTAMPTZ NULL,
    status          TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'pending'
                    CHECK (status IN ('pending', 'in_progress', 'completed', 'failed')),
    completion_note TEXT NULL,
    created_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW(),
    updated_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW()
);
```

## 4. Tenant Offboarding & Full Data Deletion

When a tenant's contract ends, the following timeline applies. Offboarding is initiated by Customer Success in Salesforce, which triggers a webhook to the `tenant-service`.

### Offboarding Timeline

```
Day 0:   Contract end date
         Tenant account marked inactive; data access suspended
         Tenant notified: data retained 90 days per DPA

Day 1-90: Data retained in full
          Tenant may request a data export via support ticket
          Export delivered as encrypted S3 presigned URL within 48 hours

Day 90:  Automated full data deletion job executes (see below)
         Completion recorded in audit_log and erasure_requests table

Day 91+: Billing records retained 7 years (financial regulation)
         PII fields in billing_records anonymized (email replaced with pseudonym)
```

### Full Deletion Job

```bash
#!/bin/bash
# scripts/tenant-offboard-delete.sh
# Usage: ./tenant-offboard-delete.sh <tenant_id>
# Requires: SRE approval + Security team sign-off recorded in Jira SEC ticket

TENANT_ID=$1

echo "=== Tenant Data Deletion: $TENANT_ID ==="
echo "Initiated by: $(whoami) at $(date -u)"

# 1. Confirm erasure request is authorized
ERASURE=$(psql $DATABASE_URL -t -c \
  "SELECT id FROM erasure_requests
   WHERE tenant_id = '$TENANT_ID'
     AND subject_type = 'tenant'
     AND status = 'in_progress';")
if [ -z "$ERASURE" ]; then
  echo "ERROR: No authorized erasure request found. Aborting."
  exit 1
fi

# 2. Delete ingestion_events in batches (avoids lock contention on large tables)
echo "[1/6] Deleting ingestion_events..."
psql $DATABASE_URL -c "
  DO \$\$
  DECLARE batch_count INT;
  BEGIN
    LOOP
      DELETE FROM ingestion_events
      WHERE id IN (
        SELECT id FROM ingestion_events
        WHERE tenant_id = '$TENANT_ID' LIMIT 10000
      );
      GET DIAGNOSTICS batch_count = ROW_COUNT;
      EXIT WHEN batch_count = 0;
      PERFORM pg_sleep(0.1);
    END LOOP;
  END \$\$;"

# 3. Drop TimescaleDB metric chunks for this tenant
echo "[2/6] Dropping metrics data..."
psql $DATABASE_URL -c \
  "DELETE FROM metrics WHERE tenant_id = '$TENANT_ID';"

# 4. Delete user sessions
echo "[3/6] Deleting user_sessions..."
psql $DATABASE_URL -c \
  "DELETE FROM user_sessions WHERE tenant_id = '$TENANT_ID';"

# 5. Anonymize billing records (retain for 7 years; remove PII)
echo "[4/6] Anonymizing billing_records PII..."
psql $DATABASE_URL -c "
  UPDATE billing_records
  SET
    contact_email = 'deleted-' || md5(contact_email) || '@anonymized.invalid',
    contact_name  = 'DELETED'
  WHERE tenant_id = '$TENANT_ID';"

# 6. Delete S3 archive for tenant
echo "[5/6] Deleting S3 archive..."
aws s3 rm "s3://aetherflow-prod-raw-event-archive/$TENANT_ID/" \
  --recursive --region us-east-1

# 7. Mark complete and soft-delete tenant record
echo "[6/6] Finalizing..."
psql $DATABASE_URL -c "
  UPDATE erasure_requests
  SET status = 'completed', completed_at = NOW(),
      completion_note = 'Full deletion completed by $(whoami)'
  WHERE tenant_id = '$TENANT_ID' AND subject_type = 'tenant';

  UPDATE tenants
  SET deleted_at = NOW(),
      admin_email = 'deleted@anonymized.invalid'
  WHERE id = '$TENANT_ID';"

echo "Tenant $TENANT_ID deletion complete."
echo "Update the Jira SEC ticket to mark it resolved."
```

> **This script is irreversible.** Two approvals are required before execution — SRE lead and Security team — both recorded in the Jira ticket. Reference the ticket number in the `completion_note`. Confirm the tenant ID carefully before running.

## 5. Automated Retention Enforcement

Manual deletion is a compliance risk. The following automated mechanisms enforce retention without human intervention.

### Nightly Deletion Job (PostgreSQL)

A Kubernetes CronJob runs at **02:00 UTC** nightly:

```yaml
apiVersion: batch/v1
kind: CronJob
metadata:
  name: data-retention-cleanup
  namespace: production
spec:
  schedule: "0 2 * * *"
  concurrencyPolicy: Forbid
  jobTemplate:
    spec:
      template:
        spec:
          restartPolicy: OnFailure
          containers:
            - name: cleanup
              image: gcr.io/aetherflow-prod/retention-cleanup:latest
              env:
                - name: DATABASE_URL
                  valueFrom:
                    secretKeyRef:
                      name: postgres-retention-job-credentials
                      key: url
              command:
                - /app/retention-cleanup
                - --dry-run=false
                - --batch-size=5000
                - --sleep-between-batches=100ms
```

The job executes these deletions in order:

```sql
-- Hard-delete expired ingestion_events
-- Uses per-tenant retention window from tenant_configurations
DELETE FROM ingestion_events
WHERE deleted_at IS NOT NULL
   OR created_at < NOW() - (
       SELECT COALESCE(event_retention_days, 90) * INTERVAL '1 day'
       FROM tenant_configurations
       WHERE tenant_id = ingestion_events.tenant_id
   );

-- Hard-delete expired user sessions
DELETE FROM user_sessions
WHERE ended_at < NOW() - INTERVAL '30 days'
   OR (ended_at IS NULL AND created_at < NOW() - INTERVAL '30 days');

-- Hard-delete churned tenant records past the 90-day window
DELETE FROM tenants
WHERE deleted_at IS NOT NULL
  AND deleted_at < NOW() - INTERVAL '90 days';
```

### TimescaleDB Automatic Retention

TimescaleDB retention policies drop old chunks automatically at the database level:

```sql
-- Applied in migration 0034_metrics_retention_policy.sql
SELECT add_retention_policy('metrics',              INTERVAL '90 days');
SELECT add_retention_policy('metric_rollups_hourly', INTERVAL '1 year');
SELECT add_retention_policy('metric_rollups_daily',  INTERVAL '3 years');
```

### S3 Lifecycle Rules (Terraform)

```hcl
# infra/environments/production/s3-lifecycle.tf
resource "aws_s3_bucket_lifecycle_configuration" "raw_event_archive" {
  bucket = aws_s3_bucket.raw_event_archive.id

  rule {
    id     = "transition-to-glacier"
    status = "Enabled"
    transition {
      days          = 90
      storage_class = "GLACIER"
    }
  }

  rule {
    id     = "expire-after-one-year"
    status = "Enabled"
    expiration { days = 365 }
  }
}
```

### Retention Job Alerting

The nightly cleanup job emits `aetherflow_retention_job_rows_deleted_total` to Prometheus. A failed run is a **SEV-2**:

```yaml
- alert: RetentionCleanupJobFailed
  expr: kube_job_status_failed{job_name=~"data-retention-cleanup.*"} > 0
  for: 1h
  labels:
    severity: sev2
  annotations:
    summary: "Nightly data retention cleanup job failed — compliance risk"
    runbook_url: "https://wiki.aetherflow.internal/policy-data-retention"
```

A failed retention job means data is not being deleted on schedule. Page the on-call SRE and the Security team lead.

## 6. Audit Logging & Retention Evidence

For SOC 2 Type II and GDPR compliance, AetherFlow must demonstrate that data is actually being deleted as promised. Evidence is collected automatically in the `audit_log` table.

Every deletion — nightly job, user erasure request, or manual offboarding — writes a record:

```sql
INSERT INTO audit_log (
    actor_type, actor_id,
    action, resource_type, resource_id,
    metadata,
    created_at
) VALUES (
    'system', 'retention-cleanup-job',
    'hard_delete', 'ingestion_events', NULL,
    '{"rows_deleted": 147823, "cutoff_date": "2023-02-09"}',
    NOW()
);
```

The `audit_log` table is:

- **Append-only** — a `BEFORE UPDATE OR DELETE` trigger raises an exception on any modification attempt
- **Monthly-partitioned** for query performance; old partitions are dropped after 2 years
- **Retained for 2 years** — non-negotiable regulatory requirement
- **Exempt from Right to Erasure** — falls under the legal obligation basis (GDPR Article 17(3)(b))

The Security team generates a quarterly Data Retention Compliance Report from `audit_log` and `erasure_requests` for the annual SOC 2 review.

## 7. Engineer Responsibilities

### When Building a Feature That Stores Data

Before merging any PR that introduces a new table, column, or data store:

- [ ] Identify the data class (Section 1) for every field you are persisting
- [ ] Determine the retention period from Section 2, or propose a new one via PR to this document
- [ ] Add any new PII fields to the inventory table in Section 1
- [ ] Confirm the nightly deletion job covers the new table/column, OR add a TimescaleDB retention policy, OR document why automated deletion does not apply
- [ ] If the feature stores data in a new storage system not covered by Section 2, get Security team sign-off before launch

### The "Where Does This Data Live?" Checklist

For any new data your feature persists, answer each of these before shipping:

```
Primary store:   Which PostgreSQL table? What retention period?
Event pipeline:  Which Kafka topic? What retention?
Cold archive:    Which S3 bucket and prefix? What lifecycle rule?
Cache:           Which Redis key pattern? What TTL?
Observability:   Logs (Loki), traces (Tempo), metrics (Prometheus)?
Third-party:     Datadog, LaunchDarkly, Stripe, SendGrid — does PII flow there?
```

"It's in Redis with no TTL" is not an acceptable answer for PII. Every store needs a retention answer.

## 8. Tribal Knowledge & Gotchas

- **Kafka is the hardest storage layer to honor erasure requests against.** Messages are immutable once written — there is no `DELETE FROM kafka WHERE user_id = X`. For GDPR compliance we rely on the 7-day default retention: by the time the 30-day erasure SLA expires, Kafka data has aged out naturally. If any team proposes extending Kafka retention beyond 30 days on a topic that may contain PII, that change requires a Security team review. It is not a routine config tweak.

- **The `audit_log` append-only trigger will surprise you in production.** If you attempt `UPDATE` or `DELETE` on `audit_log` — even as a DBA with superuser credentials — you get `ERROR: audit_log rows are immutable`. This is intentional and correct. If a row genuinely contains erroneous data, file a ticket with the Security team. There is a documented break-glass procedure for exceptional cases that requires VP of Engineering sign-off.

- **TimescaleDB chunk drops are O(1); row-level deletes are O(n).** Dropping a whole chunk is essentially a file system unlink — instant. Deleting rows with a `WHERE tenant_id = X` inside a chunk is a full scan. The nightly cleanup job is designed around chunk-aligned retention boundaries wherever possible. Per-tenant event deletion (e.g., offboarding a single tenant mid-chunk) is the slow path and can take minutes for large tenants.

- **Per-tenant retention settings live in `tenant_configurations.event_retention_days`.** Enterprise tenants can negotiate up to 2 years of event retention. If you see a large tenant's events persisting past 90 days, check their `event_retention_days` before filing a bug — it is almost certainly intentional.

- **S3 Glacier restores take 3–5 hours.** If a tenant requests a data export and their data has transitioned to Glacier (older than 90 days), the export job must issue a restore request and wait before the presigned URL can be generated. The export SLA is 48 hours specifically to accommodate this. Do not promise a same-day export for old data without first checking the S3 storage class.

- **Billing records are the exception to almost every rule.** Financial regulations mandate 7-year retention regardless of customer deletion requests or contract status. The legal basis is GDPR Article 17(3)(b) (legal obligation). The `billing_records` table has a Row-Level Security policy that prevents the nightly retention job from touching it. If a customer insists their billing records be deleted, loop in Legal before taking any action — this is a nuanced compliance question, not a simple engineering task.

- **The retention cleanup job runs at 02:00 UTC — the same window as some of our heaviest batch analytics jobs.** In Q4 2022 we had a production incident where both jobs competed for I/O on the primary PostgreSQL instance, causing P99 latency spikes across all services. The retention job now uses `pg_sleep(0.1)` between batches as a throttle. If you see the cleanup job taking > 4 hours to complete, check whether other batch jobs have been scheduled in the same window and stagger them.
